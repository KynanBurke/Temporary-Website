Lake Revival

To rejuvenate water bodies in Sarjapur and, in the process,
transform Indus DP students into climate change leaders.

Next

Background Information

Climate change poses a significant threat as the 6th mass extinction, and nowhere is this threat more prevalent than in the city of Bangalore. Bangalore has been almost entirely dependent on its extensive network of human-made lakes for fishing, irrigation, and drinking purposes with no rivers nearby. These human-made lakes, intended to be used as water reservoirs, were built periodically by the city’s rulers and have become vital to the survival of its people.

However, with the onset of Bangalore’s IT industry, lakes have become the most recent casualty of the unplanned urbanization throughout the city. In recent years, urbanization has led to the encroachment, pollution, and ultimate eradication of more than 75% of Bangalore’s original 285 lakes. 98% of the existing lakes are being encroached upon and are sewer fed today, impacting the water quality and the birds, fish, and fishers that rely on these water bodies.

Objectives Of The Project

  1. Create a thorough report detailing problems identified at selected lakes and analyzing the significance of these problems
  2. Raise awareness regarding the plight of lakes in Bangalore, attempting to enroll as many citizens into this cause
  3. Begin work on cleaning and rejuvenating these lakes, where rejuvenation entails:
    • Cleanup and filtration of the lake and surrounding area
    • Ensuring water is not toxic
    • Clearing inlets/outlets that might be clogged causing the lake to be blocked or overflow
    • Restoring the surrounding ecology at the chosen sites in an attempt to bring back the plant and animal ecosystems

Impact on School and Community

It allows us to give back to the community around us and take the first steps towards fighting climate change. In regards to the community's impact, this project will act as a measure against the negative consequences of rapid urbanization and ensure the survival of an endangered ecosystem. It aims to restore the integrity of Bangalore's depleting groundwater supply, preserve the natural habitat of countless species, and strengthen the bond between man and nature. It will help address the need for climate literacy and climate change leadership in the 21st century. Additionally, it will help members of the Indus student body develop climate literacy and a host of seldom developed skills in classrooms.
